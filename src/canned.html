Canned Food

I eat a lot of canned foods as part of my normal diet, which is something many people find bizarre about me. See below for some pictures of what I normally eat on a daily basis. There are some variations here and there based on the availability and cost of ingredients (I also try to keep my food costs reasonable without compromising on quality).

Please note that not all ingredients shown are canned, but everything is prepared with use of a microwave or hot water kettle. I also get some perishables and non-canned ingredients from websites or local grocery stores, and I eat some fresh fruits and string cheese throughout the day as a snack.

Disclaimer: I do not necessarily recommend that you follow what I do, but this diet has worked for me so far since I started around January 2022. I think canned ingredients are great for a variety of reasons, and I encourage people to use more of them as part of their daily cooking.

Breakfast

Old-fashioned oats mixed with an egg, butter, mixed nuts, roasted pumpkin seeds, chia seeds, hemp hearts, cinnamon. Dried prunes, dried figs, yogurt added as a topping. Prepared with hot water kettle.

Lunch

Whole grain English muffin with a slice of cheddar cheese, canned chicken, canned salmon, canned tuna, canned beans (assorted), canned whole kernel corn, canned carrots, red pepper flakes for seasoning. Prepared with microwave.

Dinner

Canned spinach, slice of pepperjack cheese, canned green beans, canned asparagus, canned diced tomato, canned mushrooms, canned green chilis, canned sardines, canned mackerel, hemp hearts, roasted pumpkin seeds, extra-virgin olive oil, ghost pepper (ground). Prepared with microwave.
